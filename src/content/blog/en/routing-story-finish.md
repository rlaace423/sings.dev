The last part of the series is not about adding more rules. It is about checking whether the structure still behaves well once it is in daily use.

## Design for the person operating it

Routes do their real work when something changes, breaks, or needs to be explained. That is why operational visibility matters as much as the route definition itself.

### Leave room for the next adjustment

A durable routing design is one that makes the next edit feel smaller, not riskier. That usually means keeping the structure easy to inspect, easy to trace, and easy to change without surprise.

## Closing the series

Good routing is less about the path alone and more about the reading experience it creates for the team. Once the boundaries, path splits, and operating shape are aligned, the whole system becomes calmer to evolve.
